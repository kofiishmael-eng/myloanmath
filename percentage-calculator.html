Four ways to calculate a percentage

Percentage questions come in different shapes depending on what you already know. This calculator covers the four you'll run into most often: finding a percentage of a number, finding what percentage one number is of another, finding the percentage change between two values, and applying a percentage increase or decrease to a starting number.

These four modes cover the situations that actually come up in everyday life and work, even though they look similar at a glance. "X% of Y" answers questions like calculating a tip, a discount, or a commission — you know the percentage and the base amount, and want the resulting figure. "X is what % of Y" flips that around — useful for figuring out what portion a specific number represents of a total, like what percentage of a budget was spent on one category, or what percentage of a class scored above a certain grade.

Percentage change is a different calculation entirely, and it's the one most commonly done wrong by hand. Going from 50 to 75 is a 50% increase, but going from 75 back down to 50 is not a 50% decrease — it's roughly a 33% decrease, because the base you're measuring against changed. This asymmetry trips people up constantly in contexts like stock prices, sales figures, and salary comparisons, where the direction of the comparison matters. This tool also correctly handles percentage change when the starting value is negative, which is a genuine edge case that many simple percentage calculators get backwards.

The fourth mode, applying an increase or decrease, is the reverse of percentage change — instead of comparing two known values, you start with one value and a percentage, and calculate the result. A positive percentage increases the value, a negative percentage decreases it, which lets you model things like price increases, discounts, or projected growth without needing two separate tools.

Frequently asked questions

What is 15% of 200?

Use the "X% of Y" mode with X=15 and Y=200 — the answer is 30.

How do I calculate percentage change between two numbers?

Use the "% change from X to Y" mode. It correctly handles negative starting values too, which trips up a lot of simple percentage calculators.

How do I calculate a percentage increase?

Use "Increase / decrease X by Y%" with a positive Y for an increase, or a negative Y for a decrease.
