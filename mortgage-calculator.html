What's included in this mortgage payment estimate

This mortgage calculator estimates your full monthly payment using the standard "PITI" breakdown lenders use: Principal & Interest, property Taxes, home Insurance, and — if your down payment is under 20% of the home price — Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). PMI is not charged once you've put down 20% or more, matching standard US lending practice.

Most first-time buyers underestimate their real monthly housing cost because they focus only on principal and interest — the two numbers that show up in a loan advertisement. But the actual bill a homeowner pays each month almost always includes taxes and insurance bundled in through an escrow account, and often PMI on top of that if the down payment is smaller. Leaving those out can make a home look $200-500/month more affordable than it actually is, which is exactly the gap this calculator is built to close.

PMI specifically exists to protect the lender, not the borrower — it doesn't build equity or protect your home, it simply compensates the lender if you default while you still owe more than 80% of the home's value. The moment your equity crosses that 20% threshold — either through paying down the loan or the home appreciating in value — PMI can typically be removed, which is why it's usually treated as a temporary cost rather than a permanent one. This calculator reflects the 20% cutoff accurately: cross it with your down payment, and the PMI line disappears from your estimate.

Property tax and home insurance vary enormously by location — a home in one state or county can face a property tax rate two or three times higher than an identical home elsewhere. The defaults in this calculator are rough national-average placeholders, not a prediction for your specific address. For a genuinely accurate monthly estimate, replace them with your local property tax rate (available from your county assessor's office) and an actual homeowners insurance quote.

Frequently asked questions

Why do I have a PMI charge?

PMI applies automatically whenever your down payment is under 20% of the home price — this protects the lender and is standard practice, not a fee this calculator invented.

Are the property tax and insurance numbers accurate for my area?

They start as rough placeholders. Replace them with your local property tax rate and actual insurance quote for a realistic number — these vary a lot by location.

Does the interest rate here need to be an APR?

Use the note rate (the rate on your loan estimate), not the APR, since APR bakes in fees this calculator doesn't model.
