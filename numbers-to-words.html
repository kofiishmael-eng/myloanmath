How to convert a number to words

Type a number into the box above — whole numbers, decimals, and negative numbers all work. This number to words converter reads it back the way you'd say it out loud or write it on a form: "one thousand two hundred thirty-four" instead of "1234". It supports both the international system (thousand, million, billion) and the Indian numbering system (lakh, crore), so it works whether you're writing a Western-style document or an Indian bank form.

Frequently asked questions

What is a number to words converter used for?

It's used any time you need a number written out in full — legal documents, cheques, contracts, invoices, and forms that require the amount spelled out rather than shown as digits.

Does it support the Indian numbering system?

Yes — turn on "Indian numbering" to get lakh and crore groupings (e.g. 10,00,000 as "Ten Lakh") instead of the international thousand/million/billion groupings.

Can it convert decimals and negative numbers?

Yes. Decimals are read digit-by-digit after "point" (e.g. 12.5 becomes "Twelve Point Five"), and negative numbers are prefixed with "Negative".
